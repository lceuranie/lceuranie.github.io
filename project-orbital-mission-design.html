Project · Mission Analysis

Orbital scenarios, modelled.

A series of STK studies modelling orbital mechanics, eclipse cycles, and access windows for mission-specific geometries — the analytical foundation for the mission-design coursework I've completed at ISU.

Date
2025 – 2026
Role
Sole analyst — scenario setup, propagation, reporting
Tool
STK (Systems Tool Kit) — Level 1 certified
Coverage
LEO Earth observation · GEO communications · interplanetary access geometries

01Scope

The STK work spans three families of analysis I worked through over the course of the Master of Space Studies. Each was driven by a specific mission concept under study elsewhere in the programme — the tool is most useful when the questions are concrete.

02Sun-synchronous EO scenarios

Setting up sun-synchronous orbits at the parameters typical of Sentinel-style missions (705 km altitude, ~98° inclination, descending-node local time around 10:30) and propagating to extract revisit, swath coverage, and ground-track repeat behaviour. The point is to build the intuition behind what makes EO orbits non-arbitrary — why those specific numbers, and what changes when you perturb them.

03Eclipse and power-cycle analysis

For an LEO platform, the eclipse cycle is the most consequential periodic phenomenon in the power and thermal designs. I ran eclipse-duration reports across orbital regimes (LEO ISS-like, sun-sync, MEO) to build the comparison that feeds into a CubeSat-class mission's energy-balance calculation. The output of these analyses fed directly into the EPS sizing for the Eyesat-1 prototype on this site.

04Access windows and ground-station coverage

Computing access windows from a notional spacecraft to specific ground stations (Kiruna, Svalbard, Kourou, Hartebeesthoek) for both LEO and GEO geometries. The deliverable is a per-day visibility schedule that scopes the achievable downlink budget — the same calculation, scaled up, fed into the deep-space DTE windows for the Titan swarm work.

05Deliverables

Each scenario closes with the same deliverable set: a 3D scenario file, a propagated trajectory, an access/coverage report, and a written interpretation. The interpretation is the part that matters — STK output without context is just a list of numbers. The discipline I take from this work is the same one I take from a decade of seismic interpretation: the number is not the answer; the explanation of the number is the answer.
