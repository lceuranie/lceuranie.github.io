About

From the subsurface to orbit.

An Earth Observation analyst and ten-year project leader, now training as a space systems engineer at the International Space University.

01Profile

I am Ludivine Chris Euranie — a French geophysicist and project manager currently completing the Master of Space Studies at the International Space University in Strasbourg, on a CNES scholarship. The pivot from industrial geosciences to the space sector is more continuous than it sounds: the same physics, the same engineering rigour, the same multi-stakeholder governance — applied to a different planet.

Over ten years at SLB, I delivered more than 300 geophysical studies for exploration and field development across Europe and beyond, with project revenues from $15K to $1M. I led data acquisition campaigns on hydraulic fracture monitoring and underground gas storage projects, mentored junior staff, contributed to twenty-plus technical publications, and — in my final years — directed a multi-phase information-system digital transformation across European operations that improved data integrity by 50%.

The skills that made that work successful translate directly into the space context: structured documentation, work-package coordination, risk register management, change-request workflows, audit and lessons-learned discipline. ESA programme reviews and the technical project lifecycles I ran in industry differ in vocabulary, not in substance.

02Why space, why now

Earth observation pulled me in. Working with subsurface seismic for a decade trained me to think about signal, noise, inversion, and uncertainty in three dimensions — and the modern EO stack (Sentinel-2, Google Earth Engine, machine learning, cloud-native processing) felt like an immediate application of those instincts at planetary scale. The Rwanda vegetation-monitoring app on this site grew out of that intersection: remote sensing for food security in regions underserved by dedicated assets.

From there, the path to space systems engineering was natural. The Master of Space Studies has given me hands-on training in MBSE (Cameo, plus the custom tool I built for our Titan project), orbital mission design (STK), CubeSat integration, and ECSS-style requirements engineering — alongside the policy, governance, and life-sciences foundations the programme is known for.

03What I'm looking for

An ESA internship is confirmed for August 2026 – January 2027. After that, I am open to roles in EO data processing, systems engineering, and programme management within European space missions — at agencies, primes, downstream operators, and start-ups working on Earth observation, planetary science, or in-orbit services.

Alongside the civil applications, I am specifically interested in dual-use and military space. The European policy direction over the last few years — the EU Space Strategy for Security and Defense, IRIS², the increased security envelope under Horizon Europe — describes the domain I would like to work in after the ESA internship. The Space Defense workshop project on this site is the most direct evidence of substantive engagement with that domain so far; I am building on it deliberately.

I am based in Strasbourg and willing to relocate within Europe.
