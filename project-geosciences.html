01What I did

Over ten years at SLB, I delivered geophysical analysis at a cadence that took me through nearly every variant of the work. The bulk was Vertical Seismic Profile (VSP) processing for exploration and field development: pre-processing, calibration, noise filtering, deconvolution, inversion modelling, and uncertainty analysis — the discipline of taking a seismic record and turning it into a defensible interpretation, repeatedly, across geologies that ranged from North Sea carbonates to Carpathian foreland basins.

Alongside the VSP work, I led more than 100 reservoir-characterisation and feasibility projects in the $5K – $50K bracket: 1D, 2D, 2.5D, and 3D modelling exercises that supported survey design, prospect evaluation, and well placement.

02Field operations

I led data acquisition — both on-site and remotely — on five-plus projects of Hydraulic Fracture Monitoring (HFM) and Underground Gas Storage (UGS) monitoring. These are the operations where the geophysics meets the wellhead: long days, short feedback loops, multiple stakeholders co-located, and the discipline of producing a clean dataset under operational constraints. The work taught me a kind of decision-making under pressure that no amount of office processing can substitute for.

03Commercial & mentoring

I supported more than 50 commercial proposals by translating client objectives into technical requirements — exactly the mode of working that systems engineering formalises later in this site. I mentored junior staff and contributed to 20-plus publications and technical training sessions across the European business unit.

04How this transfers

The subsurface and orbit are different volumes, but the workflow is recognisably the same: physical-model setup, propagation, data acquisition, inversion against noisy observations, uncertainty quantification, and a written interpretation that someone non-technical has to act on. The engineering rigour I learned in seismic processing translates directly to EO classification, to mission analysis, and to the systems-engineering discipline of treating every claim as a verified or unverified assertion.
